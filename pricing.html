Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before subscribing.

Where does the auction data come from? +
intelEstate sources data exclusively from official Polish judicial portals — the same government-operated databases used by courts and certified bailiffs (komornicy). Data is not scraped from secondary aggregators or third-party sites. This ensures legal compliance and data accuracy.
How often is the data updated? +
The auction database is updated nightly. New listings discovered each day are processed, scored, and available in your dashboard the following morning. The 06:00 daily digest includes all new deals added overnight, scored and ranked before most investors start their day.
How does the deal scoring algorithm work? +
Each auction is scored 0–100 using three proprietary factors: Discount depth — the gap between starting price and court-appraised value; Auction round — second-round auctions score higher because they carry deeper discounts and attract fewer bidders; Time urgency — auctions with fewer days remaining score higher. HOT deals score 85+, GOOD deals score 70–84.
What is a second-round auction and why does it matter? +
In Poland's court auction system, if a first-round auction receives no bids, the property proceeds to a second round at a lower starting price — typically two-thirds of the estimated value. Second-round auctions often have fewer bidders and deeper discounts, making them the highest-opportunity listings on the platform. intelEstate flags these automatically.
What is a deposit amount and why is it PRO-only? +
To participate in a court auction in Poland, bidders must submit a deposit (usually 10% of the starting price) before the auction date. This is critical planning information — you need to have the cash available before the auction. Deposit amounts are included in the PRO tier as part of deeper due diligence data.
How accurate are the estimated property values? +
Estimated values come directly from court-appointed appraisers (rzeczoznawcy majątkowi) — certified valuators required by Polish law. These are formal, legally-backed appraisals, not automated estimates. This is one of the key advantages of court auction data: the valuations are professional and auditable.
Can I cancel at any time? +
Yes. All plans are month-to-month with no long-term contract required. You can cancel at any time from your account settings and you'll retain access until the end of your current billing period. No cancellation fees.
Is there a money-back guarantee? +
Yes. All paid plans include a 14-day money-back guarantee. If you're not satisfied within the first 14 days, contact [email protected] for a full refund — no questions asked.
What payment methods are accepted? +
All major credit and debit cards (Visa, Mastercard), SEPA bank transfer, and PayPal. Institutional customers can request invoice billing with 30-day payment terms. All payments are processed securely and your card details are never stored on our servers.
How does annual billing work? +
Annual billing is charged once per year at a 20% discount (equivalent to 2 months free). Specialist annual is €39/mo (€468/yr), Pro annual is €119/mo (€1,428/yr). You can switch between monthly and annual billing at any renewal date.
What does the REST API provide? +
The Institutional API provides real-time JSON access to the full auction database. Key endpoints: GET /v1/auctions (list all, filterable by score, city, property type, discount), GET /v1/auctions/:id (full detail including deposit, bailiff, documents), POST /v1/alerts (custom webhook triggers), GET /v1/export (bulk CSV/JSON). Rate limits and full API documentation provided on request.
